HOMESITE INSURANCE — VEHICLE REPAIR COST REFERENCE GUIDE
Document ID: REPAIR-COSTS-001
Version: 1.0
Effective Date: January 1, 2025

This document provides repair cost reference ranges used by HomeSite Insurance
for estimating vehicle damage claims. All costs include parts and labor.
Actual costs may vary by region, repair shop, and parts availability.

1.1 BUMPER DAMAGE

Front Bumper Repair (minor dent/scratch, no replacement):
  - Economy vehicles (Honda Civic, Toyota Corolla, Hyundai Elantra): $200–$450
  - Mid-range vehicles (Honda Accord, Toyota Camry, Mazda 6): $300–$600
  - Luxury vehicles (BMW 3 Series, Audi A4, Mercedes C-Class): $500–$900
  - SUVs and Trucks (Ford F-150, Chevy Silverado, Toyota RAV4): $350–$650

Front Bumper Replacement (full replacement with sensors/camera):
  - Economy vehicles: $600–$1,200
  - Mid-range vehicles: $800–$1,600
  - Luxury vehicles: $1,500–$3,500
  - SUVs and Trucks: $900–$2,000

Rear Bumper Repair (minor dent/scratch, no replacement):
  - Economy vehicles: $200–$400
  - Mid-range vehicles: $300–$550
  - Luxury vehicles: $450–$850
  - SUVs and Trucks: $300–$600

Rear Bumper Replacement:
  - Economy vehicles: $500–$1,000
  - Mid-range vehicles: $700–$1,400
  - Luxury vehicles: $1,200–$3,000
  - SUVs and Trucks: $800–$1,800

SECTION 5: TOTAL LOSS THRESHOLD
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

A vehicle is considered a total loss when repair cost exceeds 75% of
the Actual Cash Value (ACV) of the vehicle at time of loss.

Severity Classification:
  - Minor damage:    repair cost < $1,000
  - Moderate damage: repair cost $1,000–$5,000
  - Severe damage:   repair cost $5,000–$15,000
  - Total loss:      repair cost > 75% of vehicle ACV

Standard labor rate used in above estimates: $120/hour

Regional multipliers:
  - Northeast (NY, MA, CT): 1.2x
  - West Coast (CA, WA, OR): 1.15x
  - Southeast (FL, GA, TX): 0.9x
  - Midwest (OH, IN, IL): 0.95x
  - Southwest (AZ, NV, NM): 1.0x
