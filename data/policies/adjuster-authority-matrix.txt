HOMESITE INSURANCE — ADJUSTER SETTLEMENT AUTHORITY MATRIX
Document ID: ADJUSTER-AUTHORITY-MATRIX | Version 2.0 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This matrix defines the maximum settlement each role may approve without
escalation, the rules for escalation, and where dual approval is mandatory. It
governs the "Pending Approval" stage of the claims workflow.

AUTHORITY LIMITS BY ROLE
- Adjuster: may approve settlements up to $10,000 per claim.
- Senior Adjuster (Team Lead): may approve settlements up to $50,000, and may
  approve their team members' settlements up to that limit.
- Manager / Admin: may approve settlements up to $250,000.
- Claims Committee: required for any settlement above $250,000.

SIU note: Claims in SIU Flagged status may not be settled by any role until SIU
provides a clearance or a settlement instruction, regardless of amount.

ESCALATION RULES
1. If a proposed settlement exceeds the handling adjuster's limit, the claim must
   be routed to the lowest role whose limit covers the amount.
2. Escalation requires a settlement summary and the valuation basis attached.
3. The approver reviews coverage, valuation, and documentation completeness
   before signing off. The approver may return the claim for more information.
4. An adjuster may never approve their own settlement above their personal limit
   by splitting it into multiple payments. Structuring to evade limits is a
   compliance violation.

DUAL-APPROVAL REQUIREMENTS
Two approvers (the handler's manager plus one peer manager or the committee) are
required when any of the following apply:
- Total-loss settlements at or above $35,000
- Any settlement involving a claim previously in SIU Flagged status
- Ex-gratia (goodwill) payments of any amount
- Settlements where the valuation basis deviates more than 15% from the system
  estimate

STATUS-CHANGE AUTHORITY
- Any adjuster may move their own claim forward through the normal lifecycle
  statuses (FNOL through Pending Approval).
- Moving a claim to Approved requires the settlement to be within the actor's
  authority limit or already approved by a sufficient role.
- Moving a claim to Denied requires a documented policy basis and, for injury
  claims, prior legal or supervisor review.
- Reassigning a claim to another adjuster is limited to Senior Adjuster, Manager,
  and Admin roles.

REASSIGNMENT AND WORKLOAD
Senior Adjusters may reassign claims within their team to balance workload or
match specialization. Cross-team reassignment requires Manager approval. All
reassignments must record a reason note.
