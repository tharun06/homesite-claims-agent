HOMESITE INSURANCE — AUTO POLICY MASTER TERMS
Document ID: AUTO-POLICY-MASTER | Version 4.2 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This master document defines the standard coverage terms, limits, deductibles,
and exclusions that apply to all HomeSite personal auto policies unless a
specific policy schedule or endorsement states otherwise. Adjusters should treat
this as the authoritative reference for coverage determination.

SECTION 1: COLLISION COVERAGE
Collision coverage pays for direct and accidental damage to the insured vehicle
resulting from impact with another vehicle or a stationary object, or from
vehicle rollover. Coverage applies regardless of fault. The standard collision
deductible is $500 per claim incident. Collision does not require a third party
to be involved.

SECTION 2: COMPREHENSIVE COVERAGE (OTHER-THAN-COLLISION)
Comprehensive coverage pays for loss to the insured vehicle from non-collision
causes, including theft, vandalism, fire, falling objects, hail, flood from a
covered peril, animal strike, and glass breakage. The standard comprehensive
deductible is $250 per claim incident. Glass-only claims may carry a $0
deductible if the Full Glass endorsement is present.

SECTION 3: LIABILITY COVERAGE (BODILY INJURY AND PROPERTY DAMAGE)
Liability coverage pays for injury or property damage the insured causes to
others. Standard split limits are:
- Bodily Injury (BI): $100,000 per person / $300,000 per accident
- Property Damage (PD): $50,000 per accident
Liability carries no deductible. Defense costs are paid in addition to the
limits. Liability does not cover damage to the insured's own vehicle.

SECTION 4: UNINSURED / UNDERINSURED MOTORIST (UM/UIM)
UM/UIM coverage pays for the insured's injuries and, where scheduled, property
damage caused by an at-fault driver who has no insurance or insufficient limits.
Standard UM/UIM BI limits mirror the liability BI limits ($100k/$300k). A $250
deductible applies to UM property damage claims where offered.

SECTION 5: MEDICAL PAYMENTS / PERSONAL INJURY PROTECTION (PIP)
Medical Payments (MedPay) covers reasonable medical expenses for the insured and
passengers regardless of fault, standard limit $5,000 per person. In no-fault
states, PIP replaces MedPay with statutory minimums (see STATE-COMPLIANCE
document). No deductible applies to MedPay.

SECTION 6: RENTAL REIMBURSEMENT
When the insured vehicle is undriveable due to a covered loss, rental
reimbursement pays up to $50 per day for a maximum of 30 days, ceasing when
repairs complete or a total-loss settlement is issued, whichever is first.

SECTION 7: ROADSIDE ASSISTANCE
Standard roadside covers towing to the nearest qualified repair facility, jump
starts, lockout service, flat-tire change, and up to 2 gallons of fuel delivery.
Limit of 4 service events per policy term.

SECTION 8: STANDARD EXCLUSIONS
The following are NOT covered under the base policy:
- Flood damage from external water sources not tied to a covered comprehensive peril
- Earthquake or natural-disaster damage (unless comprehensive applies)
- Acts of war, terrorism, or nuclear hazard
- Intentional or fraudulent damage caused by the insured
- Normal wear, tear, or mechanical/electrical breakdown
- Racing, speed contests, or track/competitive use
- Use of the vehicle for commercial ridesharing without the Rideshare endorsement
- Damage while the vehicle is used to carry persons or property for hire
- Custom equipment beyond $1,000 without the Custom Equipment endorsement

SECTION 9: DEDUCTIBLE SUMMARY
Collision: $500 | Comprehensive: $250 | UM Property Damage: $250
Glass with Full Glass endorsement: $0 | Liability / MedPay / PIP: none

SECTION 10: KEY DEFINITIONS
"Covered loss" means damage from a peril insured against and not excluded.
"Actual Cash Value (ACV)" means replacement cost less depreciation.
"Total loss" means repair cost plus salvage retention meets or exceeds the total
loss threshold defined in the SETTLEMENT-VALUATION guide.
