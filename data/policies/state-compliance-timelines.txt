HOMESITE INSURANCE — STATE COMPLIANCE AND RESPONSE TIMELINES
Document ID: STATE-COMPLIANCE-TIMELINES | Version 2.2 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This document summarizes state regulatory timelines and unfair-claims-practice
rules that override internal SLAs where the state figure is more protective of the
insured. When a state requirement conflicts with the internal SOP, follow the
state requirement. Timelines are calendar days unless noted.

UNIVERSAL PRINCIPLES (APPLY IN ALL STATES)
- Acknowledge a new claim promptly after FNOL.
- Investigate reasonably and pay valid claims without unreasonable delay.
- Provide a written basis when denying a claim.
- Do not misrepresent policy provisions or compel litigation by underpaying.

REPRESENTATIVE STATE TIMELINES
CALIFORNIA (West region)
- Acknowledge claim: within 15 days of notice.
- Accept or deny: within 40 days of receiving proof of claim.
- Payment after acceptance: within 30 days.

TEXAS (Southwest region)
- Acknowledge claim: within 15 days.
- Accept or deny: within 15 business days of receiving all requested items.
- Payment after acceptance: within 5 business days.

NEW YORK (Northeast region)
- Acknowledge claim: within 15 business days.
- Decision: within 15 business days after proof of loss and investigation.
- Status updates every 90 days if a claim remains open.

FLORIDA (Southeast region)
- Acknowledge claim: within 14 days.
- Pay or deny: within 90 days of notice, absent factors beyond control.

ILLINOIS (Midwest region)
- Acknowledge claim: within a reasonable time, generally 15 days.
- Provide a reasonable explanation for delay or denial in writing.

REQUIRED DISCLOSURES
- Total-loss settlements must itemize ACV, deductions, taxes, and fees.
- Denials must cite the specific policy language relied upon and the appeal path.
- When using aftermarket parts, disclose their use on the estimate where the
  state requires it.

UNFAIR CLAIMS PRACTICES (PROHIBITED)
- Failing to acknowledge or act reasonably promptly on communications.
- Denying without a reasonable investigation.
- Offering substantially less than a reasonable person would expect to recover.
- Delaying payment by requesting duplicative or unnecessary documentation.

ADJUSTER GUIDANCE
When a claim spans multiple states (e.g., loss location differs from garaging
state), apply the rules of the state with jurisdiction over the policy unless
counsel advises otherwise. When in doubt, use the shorter (more protective)
timeline and document the reasoning.
