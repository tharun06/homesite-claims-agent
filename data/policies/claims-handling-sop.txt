HOMESITE INSURANCE — CLAIMS HANDLING STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE
Document ID: CLAIMS-HANDLING-SOP | Version 3.1 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This SOP defines how an adjuster progresses an auto claim through its lifecycle,
the documentation required at each stage, and the service-level (SLA) timelines
that must be met. The claim statuses below map directly to the adjuster
dashboard.

CLAIM LIFECYCLE AND STATUSES
FNOL -> Under Review -> Investigation -> Appraisal -> Pending Approval ->
Approved / Denied -> Closed. Claims may be routed to SIU Flagged at any stage
when fraud indicators are present (see FRAUD-SIU-GUIDELINES).

STAGE 1: FNOL (FIRST NOTICE OF LOSS)
The claim is created when loss is first reported. Required at intake: policy
number, date/time and location of loss, description of the incident, involved
parties and vehicles, and police report number if applicable.
SLA: The assigned adjuster must make first contact with the insured within 1
business day (24 hours) of FNOL creation.

STAGE 2: UNDER REVIEW
The adjuster verifies coverage against the policy schedule and master terms,
confirms the policy was in force on the loss date, and validates the deductible.
Required: coverage verification note, confirmation of premium payment status.
SLA: Coverage determination within 3 business days of first contact.

STAGE 3: INVESTIGATION
The adjuster gathers evidence: recorded or written statements, photographs of all
damage, the police report, and any third-party information. For disputed-fault or
injury claims, liability is assessed here.
Required: minimum of one photograph per damaged area, a signed or recorded
statement from the insured, and a liability assessment note.
SLA: Investigation substantially complete within 10 business days of FNOL.

STAGE 4: APPRAISAL
Damage is quantified. The adjuster obtains repair estimates from a licensed
facility or assigns a field/desk appraiser. Where repair cost approaches the
total-loss threshold, a total-loss evaluation is triggered (see
SETTLEMENT-VALUATION).
Required: itemized repair estimate, photos supporting each line, total-loss
worksheet if applicable.
SLA: Appraisal/estimate completed within 5 business days of investigation close.

STAGE 5: PENDING APPROVAL
The proposed settlement is submitted for authority sign-off. The approver is
determined by the settlement amount and the adjuster's authority limit (see
ADJUSTER-AUTHORITY-MATRIX). Settlements above the handler's limit escalate.
Required: settlement summary, valuation basis, and the correct approver.

STAGE 6: APPROVED / DENIED
On approval, payment is issued per the settlement method. On denial, the adjuster
issues a written denial citing the specific policy provision or exclusion relied
upon and the insured's appeal rights.
SLA: Payment issued within 5 business days of approval. Denial letter issued
within 3 business days of the decision.

STAGE 7: CLOSED
The claim is closed after payment clears or the denial/appeal window lapses. All
documents, notes, and the final disposition must be present in the file.

DOCUMENTATION STANDARD
Every claim file must contain, before closing: coverage verification, loss
description, damage photographs, estimate or total-loss worksheet, settlement
summary, and all customer correspondence. Missing required documentation within
60 days of FNOL may result in claim denial for non-cooperation.

SLA BREACH HANDLING
A claim that misses any stage SLA is flagged as an SLA breach on the dashboard.
Breaches require a supervisor note explaining the delay and a recovery plan.
Repeated breaches on the same claim escalate to the team lead.
