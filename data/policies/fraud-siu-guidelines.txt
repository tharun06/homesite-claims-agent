HOMESITE INSURANCE — FRAUD DETECTION AND SIU REFERRAL GUIDELINES
Document ID: FRAUD-SIU-GUIDELINES | Version 2.4 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This document defines fraud red-flag indicators, how to interpret the automated
fraud score, and when and how to refer a claim to the Special Investigations Unit
(SIU). It governs use of the "SIU Flagged" status on the dashboard.

THE FRAUD SCORE
Every claim receives an automated fraud score between 0.00 and 1.00 produced by
the fraud model. The score estimates the likelihood that a claim warrants further
scrutiny. It is a triage signal, not a determination of fraud. Bands:

- 0.00 - 0.39  LOW: Handle normally. No extra fraud steps required.
- 0.40 - 0.69  ELEVATED: Enhanced review. The adjuster must document a fraud
  checklist and obtain a recorded statement before settlement.
- 0.70 - 1.00  HIGH: SIU referral required. Set the claim to SIU Flagged and do
  not issue payment until SIU clears or advises.

A claim may be referred to SIU regardless of score if red-flag indicators are
present. Score alone never justifies denial.

RED-FLAG INDICATORS
Claimant / reporting behavior:
- Loss reported significantly after the incident date without explanation
- Reluctance to provide a recorded statement or a police report
- Inconsistent or shifting accounts of the incident
- Policy purchased or coverage increased shortly before the loss

Loss characteristics:
- No independent witnesses to a significant-damage loss
- Damage inconsistent with the described mechanism of loss
- Prior unrepaired damage presented as new
- Vehicle reported stolen with no signs of forced entry and all keys accounted for
- Total-loss vehicle with recent high-value aftermarket claims

Documentation:
- Altered, duplicated, or inconsistent repair estimates or invoices
- Medical treatment patterns inconsistent with the reported impact severity
- Repeated claims involving the same shop, provider, or claimant network

SIU REFERRAL PROCESS
1. Set the claim status to SIU Flagged.
2. Complete the SIU referral note: list the specific indicators observed and
   attach supporting evidence (photos, statements, documents).
3. Place any pending payment on hold. Do not deny the claim solely on suspicion.
4. Notify the insured only as permitted; do not disclose the SIU referral itself.
5. SIU acknowledges within 2 business days and provides a disposition target.

SIU DISPOSITIONS
- Cleared: Return to normal handling; document the clearance and resume the SLA.
- Substantiated: SIU advises on denial, referral to authorities, or negotiated
  resolution. The adjuster follows SIU direction and legal review.
- Inconclusive: Proceed with enhanced documentation; note residual risk.

SCOPE AND ACCESS NOTE
SIU investigators have visibility into fraud-flagged claims across teams. Regular
adjusters see only their own book. Escalation to SIU does not transfer ownership
of the claim unless SIU explicitly assumes it.
