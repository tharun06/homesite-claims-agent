HOMESITE INSURANCE — SETTLEMENT AND VALUATION GUIDE
Document ID: SETTLEMENT-VALUATION | Version 3.0 | Effective 2026-01-01

PURPOSE
This guide explains how to value a covered loss, when a vehicle is a total loss,
and how to calculate the amount payable to the insured. It supports the Appraisal
and Pending Approval stages.

ACTUAL CASH VALUE (ACV)
ACV is the fair market value of the vehicle immediately before the loss:
replacement cost less depreciation. Determine ACV using a recognized valuation
source adjusted for the vehicle's mileage, options, prior condition, and local
market. Document the comparable vehicles or valuation report used.

REPAIRABLE LOSS PAYMENT
For a repairable vehicle, the amount payable is:
  Repair cost  -  applicable deductible  +  supplements (if approved)
The collision deductible ($500) or comprehensive deductible ($250) applies based
on the peril. Betterment may be deducted where a repair replaces a worn wear-item
(e.g., tires, battery) with new, improving pre-loss condition.

TOTAL LOSS DETERMINATION
A vehicle is a total loss when:
  Repair cost  +  net salvage retained  >=  Total Loss Threshold
The Total Loss Threshold is 75% of ACV. Some states impose a statutory total-loss
formula or a fixed percentage; where the state figure differs, use the more
protective figure for the insured (see STATE-COMPLIANCE-TIMELINES).

TOTAL LOSS PAYMENT
For a total loss, the amount payable is:
  ACV  +  applicable taxes and title/registration fees  -  deductible  -  prior
  unrelated damage  -  owner-retained salvage value (if the insured keeps the vehicle)
If the insurer retains salvage, no salvage deduction applies to the insured.

DEPRECIATION AND BETTERMENT
Depreciation applies to the vehicle as a whole for ACV, not to individual repair
parts, except for designated wear items where betterment is allowed. Never
depreciate safety-critical structural repairs.

DEDUCTIBLE WAIVER AND SUBROGATION
Where another party is clearly at fault and subrogation is pursued, the insured's
deductible is recovered on their behalf and refunded pro-rata upon recovery. The
adjuster documents the subrogation basis at settlement.

GAP AND LOAN PAYOFF
When the insured owes more than ACV on a financed vehicle and holds no GAP
endorsement, the insurer pays only ACV; the insured remains liable for the
shortfall. With the GAP endorsement, the remaining loan balance is covered up to
the endorsement limit (see ENDORSEMENTS-AND-RIDERS).

PAYMENT METHODS
Payments may be issued to the insured, jointly to the insured and lienholder, or
directly to the repair facility on a direction-to-pay. Total-loss payments on
financed vehicles are issued jointly with the lienholder to the payoff amount
first.
